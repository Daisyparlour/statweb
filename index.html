Introduction of Ayurveda

Ayurveda is a natural system of medicine originated in India more than 5,000 years ago and "Mother of all healing". Ayurveda is derived from the Sanskrit words Ayur (life) and Veda (science of knowledge). It is a system based on the understanding of the five great elements air, fire, water, earth, and ether, which are believed to be the basic components of all life, including the universe and the human body.Each of these elements has a set of particular qualities. All individuals are characterized into 3 categories based on a simplified form of the three vital energies called 'Doshas' Vatta, Pitta, and Kapha.

Ayurveda based on the idea that disease is due to an imbalance or stress in a person's consciousness, Ayurveda encourages certain lifestyle interventions and natural therapies to regain a balance between the body,mind,sprit and the environment. Ayurvada therapies start with an internal purification process, followed by a herbal remedies, massage therapy, meditation and yoga. Goals of treatment aid the person by eliminating impurities, reducing symptoms, increasing resistance to disease, reducing worry and increasing harmony in life. Herbs and other plants,including oil and common spices,are used extensively in Ayurvedic therapy.
